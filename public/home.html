Secure Multi-Party Average

Three insurers each privately enter one claim on their own page. A separate aggregator computes the average from masked shares — raw claims are never transmitted.

How it works

1. Each pair of insurers exchanges a random one-time-pad mask. For pair (i, j) with i < j, insurer i adds the mask, insurer j subtracts it.

2. Each insurer submits only s_i = x_i ± masks — a single number indistinguishable from uniform random.

3. The aggregator sums the three masked shares. Because every mask appears once with + and once with −, the masks cancel and the sum reveals x_A + x_B + x_C, from which the average follows.
