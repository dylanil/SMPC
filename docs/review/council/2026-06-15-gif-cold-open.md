# Review Council - protocol GIF comedic cold-open - 2026-06-15

**Convened:** graphics, product, ux, legal, crypto + challenger. (Not security/qa/additional-dimensions
- the change is a static teaching asset, no code/protocol/test impact.) Manager = driving session.
Owner-requested review of a proposed, rendered-but-uncommitted change.

**Proposal.** Add a four-beat "cold open" to `public/static/masks.gif` (generated by
`docs/assets/make_protocol_diagram.py`; shown on home "How it works" + README Protocol): three
colour-coded stick figures walk up; B poses the problem; A names the risk; C has the idea - then the
existing masks-cancel animation. Also: colour-coded figures beside each party circle in the averaging
beats; "Average" capitalised. Original loop ~38s.

## Opinions (one line each)
- **graphics** - support-with-changes. Clean execution; beside-circle figures slightly crowd the dense
  averaging beat; minor tail/pacing nits. No recolour, amber banner untouched.
- **product** - support-with-changes (lean trim hard). Right instinct (RB-41 wants a stronger "why"),
  but ~38s buries the strongest signal; keep the dialogue (it's Socratic, not gags).
- **ux** - support-with-changes. Comprehension win; light bubbles are a new surface so **no** contrast
  issue (FP-01/02/05 untouched); fix the mid-loop dangling caption; tighten the longest hold.
- **legal** - support-with-changes. B's and A's lines are accurate and *strengthen* honesty;
  **"encrypt" is a soft over-claim** - use "mask" to match the page's own vocabulary.
- **crypto** - support-with-changes. "encrypt … average of that" evokes homomorphic encryption, which
  this is **not**; "mask"/"hide" is accurate and lands the hook into the right mental model.
- **challenger** - NO-GO as drafted / revise to one frame. "encrypt" appears *nowhere else in the
  repo*; a ~38s looping hero has no canonical entry point and competes with the verifiability signal.

## Debate / adjudication
No relay needed: the **substantive** findings converged. (1) **Unanimous** among all who touched it
(legal, crypto, product, challenger): drop "encrypt" - it's the lone word contradicting the project's
precise "mask" vocabulary and reads as HE. (2) **Strong agreement**: ~38s is long for a hero asset and
a seamless narrative loop reads oddly mid-loop. The **only** divergence was *how hard to cut* (challenger:
one static frame; product: ≤2 beats; ux/graphics: keep the dialogue, tighten) - a creative/scope call
reserved to the owner, who had already chosen the dialogue cold-open as their vision. So the manager
adjudicated **REVISE**, not NO-GO: adopt the unanimous honesty fix, trim the intro, keep the owner's
four-beat dialogue.

## Recommendation: **REVISE**
- **Required:** `encrypt` → `mask` in C's line (honesty/accuracy).
- **Recommended:** trim the intro holds + shorten B's bubble; keep the mechanism beats at their
  owner-tuned (deliberately slow) pace.
- **Kept (owner's calls, council content with them):** B's + A's lines, the people-beside-circles,
  capital "Average". (graphics' clutter point and ux's caption-numbering point judged minor vs. the
  full-watch teaching value and the owner's approved composition.)

## Owner decision (2026-06-15): **approved REVISE.**
"mask" chosen for C's line; "trim, keep dialogue" chosen for length. Implemented (`make_protocol_diagram.py`):
C's line now "What if we could mask our data first, and take the Average of that?"; B's line tightened
to "We all want our Average - but none of us will share our number. Not with each other, and not with
any third party."; intro holds trimmed (loop ~38s → ~33.6s); mechanism pacing unchanged. GIF + still
regenerated. The numbered step-captions and beside-circle figures were kept deliberately.
