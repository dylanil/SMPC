Secure Multi-Party Average (3 Parties)

Computes the average of three private numbers using pairwise one-time-pad masks. Raw inputs never leave their party — only masked shares are aggregated.

Step 1 — Pairwise Mask Exchange

For every pair of parties, a random one-time-pad mask is jointly generated and exchanged over a secure channel. Each mask is known only to the two parties in that pair.
Masks are 64-bit random integers. They are the "pad" in one-time-pad.

Step 2 — Local Masking (each party, in isolation)

Each party computes a masked share by adding the masks from pairs where it is the lower-indexed party, and subtracting the masks from pairs where it is the higher-indexed party. The raw input x is never transmitted.

Step 3 — Aggregation of Masked Shares

The three masked shares are sent to an aggregator (or broadcast). Only these masked values are combined — the raw inputs are never seen. Because every mask appears once with a + and once with a -, all masks cancel in the sum.
